Yuhe Li

PhD Student in Economics, University of Wisconsin–Madison

I am a PhD student in Economics at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. My research interests are in the economics of artificial intelligence, empirical industrial organization, labor and the future of work, and the measurement of AI system performance and adoption.

My current work studies how generative AI changes firm strategy, model release behavior, worker productivity, and skill formation. I combine economic modeling with empirical analysis using datasets such as LMArena, Hugging Face model data, benchmark scores, and other public traces of AI development and use.

I am especially interested in research that connects empirical evidence to decisions about how AI systems should be developed, evaluated, deployed, and governed.
