Teaching

University of Wisconsin–Madison

I have taught and assisted courses in economics, data analysis, and microeconomic theory at the University of Wisconsin–Madison.

Teaching Experience

Teaching Assistant — Econ 570: Data Analytics for Economists

I help students learn Python-based data analysis for economics, including data cleaning, visualization, text analysis, APIs, regression, and reproducible workflows. I prepare discussion materials, debugging examples, coding exercises, and exam review content.

Teaching Assistant — Econ 711: Microeconomic Theory

I served as a teaching assistant for PhD Microeconomic Theory and received the Best TA Award in 2024. This role involved helping students work through formal models, proofs, and economic intuition.

My teaching emphasizes clear technical communication: translating abstract models and code into step-by-step reasoning that students can inspect, reproduce, and extend.
